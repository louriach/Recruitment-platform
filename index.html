Hiring is broken

Job boards

The hiring market is fundamentally broken, riddled with inefficiencies and systemic flaws that hinder both employers and job seekers.

Central to this dysfunction is the reliance on outdated job boards, which are ineffective for most candidates.

These platforms inundate applicants with generic job descriptions and overwhelm hiring managers with floods of resumes.

Talented designers are not given a fair shot at a career they deserve.

Progression

Career progression is another significant issue. Many organizations fail to provide clear pathways for advancement, leaving employees stagnant and disengaged.

This lack of development opportunities not only demotivates designers but stunts innovation and growth within companies.

Talented individuals are often forced to seek new positions elsewhere, perpetuating a cycle of turnover and instability.

Referrals

The industry's heavy dependence on referrals exacerbates these problems, creating an uneven playing field. While networking can open doors, it also reinforces biases and limits diversity. Those without access to influential networks face significant disadvantages, regardless of their qualifications or potential. This gatekeeping mechanism perpetuates inequality, as opportunities are often reserved for those within certain social circles.

To address these issues, we must innovate hiring practices, emphasize transparent career growth, and develop fairer systems that value merit over connections. Only then can we create a more efficient, equitable job market for all.

Change

We want to try and fix this.

We're not 100% sure how yet, but believe that the CV is in need of reinvention. Maybe they are the problem?

We have a working theory that job matching is the future. Bye bye to blind applications.

Imagine being told that a company fits your profile, rather than hoping that you pass an algorithmic system's judgement.
